Location

The ceremony and reception will both be held at Glen Magna Farms in Danvers, Massachusetts on Sunday, May 12, 2013. Guests should prepare to arrive at [5:30]pm sharp!

Directions:

  • The official address is 29 Ingersoll St, Danvers, MA. If you are using a GPS and it does not recognize this address, use 83 Centre St Danvers, MA. At this corner there is a sign for Glen Magna Farms. Once you are headed down the long road, keep driving until you see the house! You will know when you arrive.
  • From Logan Airport, follow signs for Massachusetts 1A N/Revenue and merge onto I-90E. Continue on 1A until Beach St and Vfw Pkwy, and take the slight left onto MA-60W. At the first traffic circle, continue straight on MA-60W. At the second traffic circle, take the 1st exit onto the US-1N ramp to Newburyport. After 10.4 miles, take the Centre St exit. Turn left onto Centre St, and take the 3rd left onto Ingersoll Street.
  • From I-95N (coming from CT), take exit 44B towards US-1N/Danvers. Continue on US-1N/Newburyport Turnpike and keep right at forks. Take the Centre St exit. Turn left onto Centre St, and take the 3rd left onto Ingersoll Street.

Things to Do

If you arrive early or for the weekend, spend the day in Boston or Cambridge! We recommend:

  • Visiting MIT, where we met, if you have not been there before or failed to spend much time off-campus during your years there (like Yalu) or if you have not visited recently (there have been a lot of campus changes!)
  • Visiting that other school down the road
  • Spending some time in the Boston Commons (park) or Gardens (which should be blooming in May!)
  • See the renowned Museum of Science or Museum of Fine Arts or Institute of Contemporary Art or New England Aquarium or Boston Public Library or the Mapparium
  • Go beer tasting at Harpoon (our personal favorite) or Sam Adams
  • Catch a ballgame at Fenway
  • See historic Boston by walking the Freedom Trail
  • Any recommendations by The New York Times or Time Travel

We have done all of the above and more, so don't hesitate to ask for advice or recommendations
