Why 5-12-13

As mentioned in How we met, Yalu and Mike's first "date" was a showing of Fermat's Last Tango with a group of friends.
Fermat's Last Tengo is a play about solving Fermat's last theorem, which can by simply stated as: 

an + bn = cn does not have any integral solutions where n > 2.
In the case where n = 2, then we have the pythagorean theorem. Integral solutions are called pythagorean triples, and 5, 12, 13 is a common triple that is used throughout high school.

Things to Do

Around Boston

If you arrive early or for the weekend, spend the day in Boston or Cambridge! We recommend:

  • Visiting MIT, where we met, if you have not been there before or if you have not visited recently (there have been a lot of campus changes!)
  • Visiting that other school down the road
  • Spending some time in the Boston Commons (park) or Gardens (which should be blooming in May!)
  • See the renowned Museum of Science or Museum of Fine Arts or Institute of Contemporary Art or New England Aquarium or Boston Public Library or the Mapparium
  • Go beer tasting at Harpoon (our personal favorite) or Sam Adams
  • Catch a ballgame at Fenway
  • See historic Boston by walking the Freedom Trail
  • Any recommendations by The New York Times or Time Travel

Around Danvers

  • Salem, Massachusetts. Your visit will likely be a lot more calmer and peaceful than when Mike and Yalu visited on Halloween a few years ago.
  • Crane Beach in Ipswich is a beautiful, sandy beach (rare in Massachusetts)! You can lie on the beach, hike on the trails, or visit Castle Hill.
  • If you are feeling adventurous, we strongly recommend Plum Island Kayaking. It's a huge amount of fun, no matter what type of kayaking trip you go on. We have done the moonlight kayak and the seal kayak, but our most memorable incident was when Lucy and Yalu shared a tandem and got beached in a huge storm (the whole group had to go back — it was too windy that day!)
  • Get wiped out at the Wipeout Obstacle Course, opening Fall 2012 in Danvers.
